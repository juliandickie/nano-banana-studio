# Video Domain Modes Reference

> Load this when selecting a domain mode for video generation or when the
> user asks about video styles, shot types, or camera setups.

## 6 Video Domain Modes

### Product Reveal

**Use case:** E-commerce launches, product showcase, unboxing
**Camera:** Slow dolly in + 45-degree orbit, macro close-ups for detail
**Lighting:** Studio softbox, clean rim separation, reflective surface catches
**Duration:** 8s (full reveal arc)
**Audio:** Subtle ambient hum, SFX on product interaction (click, snap, whoosh)

**Camera vocabulary:** dolly in, orbit, macro push, hero angle, three-quarter rotation, spotlight sweep
**Surface vocabulary:** polished marble, brushed concrete, gradient sweep, floating platform
**Example prompt opener:** "Slow dolly forward toward [PRODUCT] resting on [SURFACE], camera orbiting 45 degrees to reveal..."

### Story-Driven

**Use case:** Brand narrative, testimonials, lifestyle, emotional connection
**Camera:** Tracking shots following subject, rack focus between characters, over-the-shoulder
**Lighting:** Natural/practical, motivated by scene (window light, neon signs, campfire)
**Duration:** 8s (one complete beat of story)
**Audio:** Dialogue in quotes, ambient soundscape, emotional music bed

**Camera vocabulary:** tracking, Steadicam, rack focus, pull focus, dolly alongside, whip pan
**Character vocabulary:** identity-locked descriptors, wardrobe lock, expression progression
**Example prompt opener:** "[CHARACTER DESCRIPTION] walks through [SETTING], camera tracking alongside as they..."

### Environment Reveal

**Use case:** Real estate, architecture, travel, location showcase
**Camera:** Drone/crane establishing shots, slow tilt reveals, push-through doorways
**Lighting:** Golden hour, blue hour, dramatic sky, architectural lighting
**Duration:** 8s (full location reveal)
**Audio:** Ambient environmental sounds (birdsong, waves, city hum), no dialogue

**Camera vocabulary:** aerial drone, crane ascending, push-through, reveal, establishing, orbit wide
**Environment vocabulary:** atmospheric perspective, depth layers, foreground framing, volumetric light
**Example prompt opener:** "Aerial drone shot descending slowly over [LOCATION], revealing [FEATURE] as the camera pushes forward..."

### Social Short

**Use case:** TikTok, Reels, Shorts, social ads, punchy content
**Camera:** Dynamic handheld, fast rack focus, jump cuts implied by energy
**Lighting:** High contrast, neon, bold colors, energetic
**Duration:** 4s (one punchy beat)
**Audio:** Punchy SFX, trending music style, short dialogue hook

**Camera vocabulary:** handheld, snap zoom, whip pan, dynamic rack focus, POV
**Energy vocabulary:** fast-paced, energetic, bold, attention-grabbing, scroll-stopping
**Example prompt opener:** "Dynamic handheld close-up of [SUBJECT] [QUICK ACTION], fast rack focus to..."

### Cinematic

**Use case:** Film trailers, mood pieces, brand anthems, premium content
**Camera:** Steadicam, anamorphic lens references, deliberate slow movements
**Lighting:** Chiaroscuro, dramatic Rembrandt, split light, atmospheric haze
**Duration:** 8s (atmospheric, deliberate pacing)
**Audio:** Cinematic score swell, deep ambient bass, dramatic SFX

**Camera vocabulary:** Steadicam glide, anamorphic flare, Dutch angle, low-angle hero, crane down
**Film references:** RED V-Raptor, ARRI Alexa 65, Cooke S7/i anamorphic, Kodak Vision3 500T
**Example prompt opener:** "Steadicam glide through [SETTING], anamorphic lens flares catching [LIGHT SOURCE]..."

### Tutorial/Demo

**Use case:** How-to, UI walkthrough, educational, product demo
**Camera:** Static tripod or slow pan, clean framing, screen-capture style
**Lighting:** Even, well-lit, minimal shadows, clarity over mood
**Duration:** 6s (one step or demonstration)
**Audio:** Calm narration voice, subtle click/tap SFX, minimal ambient

**Camera vocabulary:** locked-off tripod, slow pan, overhead flat lay, screen recording style
**Clarity vocabulary:** clean, well-organized, step-by-step, clearly visible, labeled
**Example prompt opener:** "Overhead flat-lay shot of [SUBJECT] as hands [DEMONSTRATE ACTION], clearly showing..."

## Shot Types for Multi-Shot Sequences

When building sequences (15s+), each shot in the shot list should specify a cinematographic shot type designed for editorial cutting:

| Shot Type | Purpose | Duration | Camera |
|-----------|---------|----------|--------|
| **Establishing wide** | Opens a scene, sets location | 4-6s | Static or slow pan |
| **Medium shot** | Main action, conversation | 6-8s | Tracking or static |
| **Close-up** | Emotion, detail, product focus | 4-6s | Static or slow dolly |
| **Extreme close-up** | Texture, micro-detail, tension | 4s | Static, macro |
| **Cutaway/Insert** | Detail shot for edit transitions | 4s | Static, tight framing |
| **Reaction shot** | Character response (dialogue scenes) | 4-6s | Static medium |
| **Over-the-shoulder** | Conversation coverage | 6-8s | Static or slight drift |
| **B-roll** | Supplementary footage for flexibility | 4-8s | Handheld or drone |
| **Transition shot** | Visual bridge between scenes | 4s | Dolly, whip pan |
| **POV shot** | First-person perspective | 4-6s | Handheld with motion |

### Editing Rhythm Guide

| Pace | Avg Shot Length | Style |
|------|----------------|-------|
| **Fast** (social ads) | 2-4s | Quick cuts, energy, attention |
| **Medium** (brand films) | 4-6s | Balanced, narrative flow |
| **Slow** (cinematic, luxury) | 6-8s | Deliberate, atmospheric |

### Target Durations

| Target | Shots | Structure |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| **15s** (social ad) | 2-3 | Establish → Action → Close |
| **30s** (standard ad) | 4-5 | Establish → Problem → Solution → Product → CTA |
| **60s** (brand film) | 8-10 | Full narrative arc with B-roll |
| **90s** (product demo) | 12-15 | Tutorial structure with cutaways |
| **2min+** (mini-doc) | 16-20 | Multi-scene with transitions |
